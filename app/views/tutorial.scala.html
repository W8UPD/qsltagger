How does tagging work?

When you are ready to begin tagging, you'll be presented with a random QSL card. You'll be shown all sides of the card, and asked to type information relating to it.

Some things you should tag are callsign, location, time and date of the contact, signal reports, and any other information that could help people search. The information that you input will be used to create a searchable index, which will let people easily query our database to find matching QSL cards, given a set of searchable criteria.

So how do I do it?

Well, go to the home page. You'll be presented with a random QSL card, for you to tag. To get started, click and drag over an area of the card that you wish to tag. For example, draw a box over the callsign that appears on a card. A modal dialog will come up, and give you options, which will allow you to tag that piece of information appropriately.
